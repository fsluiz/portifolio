Ph.D. in Theoretical Physics
2010 - 2014

Ph.D. in Theoretical Physics from the Physics Department of the Federal University of São Carlos.

During my Ph.D., I studied one of the most surprising applications of quantum mechanics that completely escape our common sense: quantum teleportation in continuous variables. Based on data analysis of coherent states, we propose modifications in its construction to improve the efficiency of the teleportation protocol. These modifications were made in order to take into account the realistic situations found in the implementation of the protocol, that is, the fact that the entanglement, between Alice and Bob, is never perfect and the fact that the states teleported by Alice are close to the vacuum state. After this analysis, we performed an extension of the teleportation protocol proposing a multichannel protocol and studied the case of two channels in parallel. Finally, we created the first quantum cryptography protocol that actively uses continuous variable teleportation. We show that this protocol is robust to the beamsplitter attack and is the only one that works with direct reconciliation and without post-selection in the 50% loss scenario.

Master's Degree in Theoretical Physics.
2008 - 2010

Master's Degree in Theoretical Physics from the Federal University of Uberlândia.

During my master's degree, I performed the analysis of a Bose-Einstein condensate of rubidium atoms in two hyperfine levels coupled by a two-photon transition. Analysis of the dynamics data of this system showed that control over the coupling of the condensate allows the creation of a set of universal quantum logic gates, which are the fundamental ingredient for quantum computing. Showing the viability of the Bose-Einstein condensate as a physical system for quantum computing.
